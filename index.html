About Me

I first caught coding vibes in 2001 when I was 11 years old and my brother brought over a copy of the original Fallout game for PC. For a few months I was addicted to fighting radscorpions and raiders, relying on my trusty Pip-boy for survival. He took the game back to his mother's eventually but I had gained an affinity for Courier New and green text on a dark gray background. A few years later I was introduced to Gaia Online by my school friends, an anime themed online web forum where I exercised my creative writing skills by participating in and creating text-based roleplaying games. It was there that I memorized BB Code and felt the satisfaction of formatting beautiful posts by simply framing my text with code.

It was also the days of myspace and sometimes I browsed all the profile templates created by other people, wondering how they did that. I looked at the code and it was almost readable, but not readable enough for me to consider that I would be able to do something like that.

Those days ended, years passed, and I worked towards going into a helping and healing profession, working between administrative, customer service and therapy aide roles until I eventually became a Licensed Massage Therapist in 2018. I didn't give coding much more thought until COVID hit in 2020 and I started trying to think of ways to distance myself from the close contact with clients that was necessary for my profession. I looked into free Harvard courses during that time-one of them being CS50-but I had many other things to figure out before I could focus on anything new.

By 2023, I reached a point in my life where I felt that I could no longer sustain the level of care that I provided to others while maintaining my own health and wellness at the same time. I searched for a change and I remembered the old days I spent on webforums, playing Fallout, and forgetting about the real world.

I knew learning to code would be a challenge because it would be a radical change to the skillset that I had cultivated for over 10 years, but I was ready for a lifestyle in which I could focus on myself and my family rather than draining myself trying to heal the whole world. A remote position as a web developer answers that need.

When I'm not at my desk, I'm usually outside in nature, with my family, obsessing over plants or snuggling with my animals.
